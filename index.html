Game Log

How to Play

🎯 The Goal

Race to the target score (usually 200). You score points by making the open ends of the board add up to a multiple of 5. That's it. Simple math, deep strategy.

🦴 Your Tiles

28 dominoes, each with two numbers (0–6). You get 7 tiles with 2 players, 5 tiles with 3–4. The rest go in the boneyard (draw pile).

🏁 How It Starts

Whoever has the highest double (6|6, then 5|5, etc.) plays it first. This tile becomes the spinner — the center of everything.

🎮 On Your Turn

Match one of your tiles to an open end on the board. No match? Draw from the boneyard until you can play. Boneyard empty? Pass.

⭐ The Spinner (Key Concept)

The spinner can be played on all 4 sides. North and south open up once left and right each have a tile. That means up to 4 open ends for scoring.

💰 Scoring

After you play, add up all open ends. If the total is divisible by 5, you score that many points:

5 · 10 · 15 · 20 · 25 · 30...

🔥 Doubles count both halves. A 4|4 at the end = 8, not 4. This is huge for scoring.

🏆 Round Over

Someone plays their last tile? "Domino!" — they win the round and get bonus points from everyone else's leftover pips. If nobody can move, the player with the fewest pips wins.

🤝 Teams (2v2)

You + an AI partner vs two opponents. Shared score. Your partner's leftover pips get subtracted from the bonus, so teamwork matters.

💡 Pro Tips

🧮 Count the ends before every play — know the sum.
🎲 Play doubles early — they're hard to get rid of later.
🌈 Keep variety — different numbers = fewer draws.
🚫 Watch who draws — they're missing something. Exploit it.
💡 Use hints — the AI will suggest the best move (costs 5 pts).
