All Fives Dominoes

🎲 History

Dominoes originated in China around the 12th century and traveled to Europe through Italy in the 18th century. All Fives — also known as Muggins — emerged as one of the most popular American domino games, especially beloved in the Southern United States, Caribbean, and Latin America. It's a game of skill, strategy, and counting that rewards players who can think ahead while keeping track of what's been played.

🎯 Objective

Be the first player (or team) to reach the target score (typically 200 points). Points are earned during play by making the exposed ends of the domino layout add up to a multiple of 5 — hence the name "All Fives."

🦴 The Tiles

Uses a standard double-6 set containing 28 unique tiles, each showing two pip values from 0 to 6. Every possible combination appears exactly once. With 2 players, each draws 7 tiles. With 3-4 players, each draws 5 tiles. The remaining tiles form the boneyard (draw pile).

🏁 Starting a Round

The player holding the highest double (6-6, then 5-5, etc.) must play it first — this is mandatory. If no player has a double, the tiles are reshuffled and redealt. The first double played becomes the spinner.

⭐ The Spinner

The spinner is the most important tile on the board. It sits at the center and can eventually be played on all four sides (left, right, north, south). The north and south arms open once both the left and right sides have at least one tile. This creates a cross-shaped layout with up to 4 open ends for scoring.

💰 Scoring — The Heart of the Game

After placing a tile, add up the pip values on all open ends of the layout. If the total is a multiple of 5, you score that many points immediately:

5 pts · 10 pts · 15 pts · 20 pts · 25 pts...

Critical rule: When a double is at an open end, both halves count. A 4-4 at the end counts as 8 toward the sum, not 4. This makes doubles extremely valuable for scoring!

🦴 Drawing & Passing

If you can't match any open end, you must draw tiles from the boneyard one at a time until you find a playable tile. If the boneyard is empty and you still can't play, you pass your turn. Strategic players try to avoid drawing by keeping a diverse hand.

🏆 End of Round

A round ends when someone plays their last tile ("Dominoes!") or the game is blocked (no player can make a move). The winner receives bonus points equal to the total pips remaining in opponents' hands, rounded to the nearest multiple of 5.

🥇 Winning the Game

The first player or team to reach the target score wins. If a player reaches the target during play (from a scoring move), the game ends immediately — you don't have to wait for the round to finish.

🤝 Team Play (2v2)

In team mode, you and an AI partner sit across from each other against two AI opponents. Partners share a combined score. When a teammate dominoes, the partner's remaining pips are subtracted from the bonus — so going out with a heavy-handed partner can reduce your reward.

💡 Strategy Tips

• Count the ends — Always know the current sum before you play.
• Play doubles early — They're harder to play later and count double at the ends.
• Diversify your hand — Keep tiles with different values to avoid drawing.
• Block opponents — If you notice an opponent drawing repeatedly, they're missing certain values. Play those values to keep them stuck.
• Use the Hint — The 💡 button uses AI analysis to suggest the best move (costs 5 points).
