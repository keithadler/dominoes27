Game Log

All Fives Rules

Objective

Be the first player (or team) to reach the target score. Points are earned during play by making the open ends of the layout add up to a multiple of 5.

Setup

Uses a standard double-6 domino set (28 tiles). With 2 players, each draws 7 tiles. With 3-5 players, each draws 5. Remaining tiles form the boneyard.

Starting Play

The player holding the highest double (6-6, then 5-5, etc.) plays it first. This is automatic. If no one has a double, tiles are reshuffled and redealt.

The Spinner

The first double played becomes the spinner. It can be played on all four sides. The north and south arms open once both left and right sides have tiles.

Scoring During Play

After placing a tile, add up all open ends of the layout. If the sum is a multiple of 5 (5, 10, 15, 20...), you score that many points.

Doubles at an open end count both sides. A 4-4 at the end counts as 8, not 4.

Drawing & Passing

If you can't play, you must draw from the boneyard until you get a playable tile. If the boneyard is empty, you pass.

End of Round

A round ends when someone plays their last tile ("dominoes") or the game is blocked (nobody can play). The winner gets bonus points from opponents' remaining tiles, rounded to the nearest 5.

Winning

First player or team to reach the target score wins the game.

Hint System

Press the 💡 Hint button during your turn to see the AI-recommended best move. Costs 5 points. Uses minimax with alpha-beta pruning to find the optimal play.

Teams (2v2)

In team mode, you and an AI partner sit across from each other against two AI opponents. Team scores are shared. When a teammate dominoes, the partner's remaining pips are subtracted from the bonus.
